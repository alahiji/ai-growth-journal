## Phase 4: Tax Setup (Within 30 Days)

- [ ] **Register with Texas Comptroller** for Franchise Tax
  - [Texas Comptroller WebFile](https://comptroller.texas.gov/taxes/franchise/)
  - As a new LLC, you'll file a **No Tax Due Report** if revenue < $2.47M
- [ ] **Note first franchise tax deadline**: May 15, ________
- [ ] **Determine if you need sales tax permit**
  - If selling tangible goods or taxable services in Texas → YES
  - If selling only non-taxable services (consulting, software development) → likely NO
  - [Texas Sales Tax Info](https://comptroller.texas.gov/taxes/sales/)
- [ ] **Set up accounting/bookkeeping**
  - Recommended: QuickBooks Self-Employed, Wave (free), or FreshBooks
  - Track ALL income and expenses from day one

## Phase 5: Business Operations Setup (Week 2-4)

### Domain & Online Presence
- [ ] **Register business domain** (e.g., luigiai.com, luigi-ai.com)
- [ ] **Set up business email** (e.g., you@luigiai.com)
- [ ] **Create basic website/landing page** (if applicable)

### Business Tools
- [ ] **Set up invoicing system** (QuickBooks, FreshBooks, Wave, etc.)
- [ ] **Create standard contract/service agreement template** (for client work)
- [ ] **Set up payment processing** (Stripe, PayPal Business, etc.)

### Insurance (Evaluate Need)
- [ ] **General liability insurance** — consider if doing client-facing work
- [ ] **Professional liability / E&O insurance** — recommended for consulting/AI services
- [ ] **Cyber liability insurance** — consider if handling client data

---

## Phase 6: Local Compliance (Week 2-4)

- [ ] **Check city business license requirements**
  - Contact your city's business licensing office
  - Some Texas cities require a general business license or home occupation permit
- [ ] **Check county requirements** (if any)
- [ ] **Check zoning compliance** if operating from home
  - Most home-based consulting/software businesses are fine
  - Issues arise mainly with signage, customer traffic, or inventory storage
