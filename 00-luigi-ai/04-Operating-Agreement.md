# Operating Agreement of Luigi AI LLC

## A Texas Limited Liability Company

---

> **IMPORTANT**: This is a template for a single-member LLC operating agreement. While Texas does not require you to file this with the state, having an operating agreement is **strongly recommended** to:
> - Protect your limited liability status
> - Establish clear rules for the LLC
> - Separate your personal and business affairs
> - Satisfy bank requirements when opening a business account
>
> **Print, fill in the blanks, sign, and keep with your LLC records.**

---

## ARTICLE III — MANAGEMENT

**3.1 Member-Managed.** The Company shall be managed by its Member(s). The sole Member shall have full and exclusive authority to manage the business and affairs of the Company.

**3.2 Powers of the Member.** The Member shall have the power to:
- (a) Enter into contracts and agreements on behalf of the Company
- (b) Open and manage bank accounts in the Company's name
- (c) Hire employees and independent contractors
- (d) Purchase, lease, or sell assets
- (e) Borrow money and incur obligations
- (f) Take any and all actions necessary to carry out the purposes of the Company

**3.3 Limitation of Liability.** The Member shall not be personally liable for the debts, obligations, or liabilities of the Company solely by reason of being a Member.

---

## ARTICLE V — ALLOCATIONS AND DISTRIBUTIONS

**5.1 Profits and Losses.** All profits and losses of the Company shall be allocated to the sole Member.

**5.2 Distributions.** Distributions shall be made to the sole Member at such times and in such amounts as the Member determines, subject to the Company's obligations and applicable law.

**5.3 Tax Treatment.** As a single-member LLC, the Company shall be treated as a **disregarded entity** for federal income tax purposes unless the Member elects otherwise. All income, deductions, and credits of the Company shall be reported on the Member's personal tax return (Schedule C of Form 1040).

---

## ARTICLE VI — RECORDS AND ACCOUNTING

**6.1 Books and Records.** The Company shall maintain complete and accurate books and records of all Company transactions at the principal office.

**6.2 Fiscal Year.** The fiscal year of the Company shall be the calendar year (January 1 – December 31).

**6.3 Bank Accounts.** The Company shall maintain one or more bank accounts in the Company's name, separate from the Member's personal accounts. All Company funds shall be deposited in such accounts.

---

## ARTICLE VII — TRANSFER OF MEMBERSHIP INTEREST

**7.1 Restrictions on Transfer.** The Member may freely transfer all or any portion of the membership interest. Upon transfer of 100% of the membership interest, the transferee shall be admitted as a substitute Member.

---

## ARTICLE VIII — DISSOLUTION

**8.1 Events of Dissolution.** The Company shall be dissolved upon:
- (a) The written decision of the Member to dissolve the Company
- (b) The entry of a judicial decree of dissolution
- (c) Any event that makes it unlawful for the Company to continue

**8.2 Winding Up.** Upon dissolution, the Member shall wind up the Company's affairs, pay all debts and obligations, and distribute remaining assets to the Member.

**8.3 Certificate of Termination.** Upon completion of winding up, the Member shall file a Certificate of Termination with the Texas Secretary of State.

---

## ARTICLE IX — INDEMNIFICATION

**9.1 Indemnification.** The Company shall indemnify the Member and any agents of the Company to the fullest extent permitted by the Texas Business Organizations Code against any losses, claims, damages, or expenses arising from actions taken in good faith on behalf of the Company.

---

## ARTICLE X — MISCELLANEOUS

**10.1 Governing Law.** This Agreement shall be governed by the laws of the State of Texas.

**10.2 Amendments.** This Agreement may be amended only by a written instrument signed by the Member.

**10.3 Severability.** If any provision of this Agreement is held to be invalid or unenforceable, the remaining provisions shall remain in full force and effect.

**10.4 Entire Agreement.** This Agreement constitutes the entire agreement regarding the Company and supersedes all prior agreements and understandings.
